Flashlight

  • High Concept: Find the MacGuffin whilst being pursued by a spoopy ghost, with a really short-lasting battery.
  • Player Experience Goals: They're supposed to be spooked, with a sense of impending doom.
  • Genre: Survival Horror
  • Game play: The player may move left, right, up, or down in a manner similar to other top-down games using the arrow keys. The spacebar activates/deactivates the flashlight. Ostensibly, the flashlight would be able to banish evil spirits, but I didn't get to that feature. Oh well.
  • Prototype goal: A flashlight with battery life, along with a pursuing enemy and proximity-based sound effects.
  • Features: Experience true fear as a 81x81 ghost descends upon you! Realistic non-functioning flashlight!
  • Setting/Premise: It's supposed to be a normal, modern setting with some deep, dark things hiding in the shadows. Ghosts are real, and they are wrathful, murderous, and full of suffering. It is well that a ghost is a rare thing to come across, because God help you if you stumble upon one.
  • Story: A man finds his brother's journal one cold spring night; it speaks of his brother's house and the horrors he found within. The man decides to investigate
  • Target Audience: The type of guy who plays Amnesia:Dark Descent and other spooky games. Horror fans, basically.
  • Play time: Around 30 seconds, depending on how conservative you are with battery life.
  • Strategies: I'll be the first to admit that this isn't the most complete game around, so strategy isn't really a factor, here. Most of my time was spent implementing the flashlight business and getting the assets setup.
  • Prototype Assets: All sprites came from spriters-resource.com. Breathing sound effect from soundbible.com 'Aftermath' by Kevin MacLeod, incompetech.com
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: I borrowed some movement code from the StarStruck example, and took inspiration from the audio, timer, and masking examples. This would be most similar to a little known game called Ao Oni, (JAP: "Blue Demon"). Like that game, I'm going for a top-down horror sort of game.
